# Local Networking Script

## Short intro

Hi, I’m building Harbor Path with my wife. She is on the autism care side, and I’m building the product and business. We’re starting locally on Long Island with a simple goal: help families find a clearer first step into virtual ABA support from home.

## How to explain it

Harbor Path is an early-stage virtual autism care platform for New York families. The care is clinician-led, and AI helps with intake, progress tracking, summaries, and caregiver guidance. We’re starting with Long Island families first before expanding.

## What to ask community organizations

We’re not trying to replace local community support. We want to learn from families and partner responsibly. What are parents struggling with most right now when they try to access ABA or caregiver support?

## Good questions to ask

- What do families on the East End need most after diagnosis?
- Where are the biggest access gaps?
- Are families open to virtual caregiver coaching?
- Do families struggle more with finding providers, insurance, scheduling, or understanding next steps?
- Would a clinician-led virtual ABA starting point be useful?
- What would make you trust a new care platform?

## What to offer

- Free parent education webinar
- Resource guide for starting ABA in New York
- Caregiver coaching information session
- Warm referral process when Harbor Path is ready
- Clinician recruiting partnership if they know LBAs/BCBAs

## What not to do

- Do not hard sell families at community events.
- Do not promise insurance coverage until confirmed.
- Do not claim AI provides therapy.
- Do not collect sensitive medical details through casual forms.
- Do not offer referral payments to healthcare/community partners without legal review.
