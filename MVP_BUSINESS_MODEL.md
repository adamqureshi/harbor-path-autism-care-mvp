# Harbor Path MVP Business Model

## One-line business

Harbor Path helps New York families start virtual ABA support from home through licensed clinicians, caregiver coaching, and AI-assisted progress tools.

## MVP wedge

Start local before national.

Target first:

- Long Island families, especially Bellport, South Shore, Southampton, East Hampton, Water Mill, Manorville, and nearby East End communities
- Parents looking for a clearer first step into ABA care
- New York LBAs/BCBAs interested in flexible virtual work
- Local autism organizations, pediatric practices, schools, and family groups

## Who pays?

Phase 1: Demand validation

- Families join early access
- Clinicians join provider interest list
- Harbor Path validates need, trust, price sensitivity, and provider availability

Phase 2: Paid concierge / caregiver coaching pilot

- Private-pay intake and care navigation
- Parent/caregiver coaching packages where legally and clinically appropriate
- Clinician-led sessions delivered by qualified providers

Phase 3: Insurance / payer-ready care model

- Credential clinicians or group practice
- Add payer contracting, eligibility checks, prior authorization workflows, claim submission, documentation standards, and compliance operations

## Revenue model options

Do not think of this as simply “taking a cut” from a provider. In healthcare, especially in New York, fee splitting, referrals, Medicaid, and professional entity rules matter.

Possible compliant structures to review with a healthcare attorney:

1. **Professional practice model**
   - A properly structured professional entity bills families or payers.
   - Harbor Path pays clinicians as employees or contractors.
   - Margin comes from billed revenue minus clinician compensation, software, admin, and compliance costs.

2. **Management services model**
   - A professional ABA practice provides care.
   - Harbor Path provides non-clinical services: marketing, software, intake, scheduling, documentation tooling, provider operations.
   - Harbor Path charges a fair-market management/admin fee.

3. **Private-pay starter product**
   - Families pay for an intake/readiness/caregiver coaching package.
   - This validates demand before full insurance operations.

4. **Provider SaaS / workflow layer later**
   - Clinicians or small practices pay for AI-assisted intake, documentation, caregiver plan templates, and progress tracking.
   - This is a later B2B expansion after you understand clinical workflows.

## MVP success metrics

Demand metrics:

- 100+ family landing page visitors
- 20+ family early-access submissions
- 10+ intro calls booked
- 5+ families willing to pay or proceed to care navigation

Supply metrics:

- 10+ clinician interest submissions
- 3–5 qualified NY LBAs/BCBAs willing to pilot
- 1–2 clinicians ready for first families

Trust metrics:

- Parent response to the brand
- Local partner introductions
- Clinical advisor feedback
- Ability to explain compliance clearly

Business metrics:

- Cost per family lead
- Family conversion rate
- Provider match rate
- Gross margin per session or package
- Admin hours saved by AI

## 30 / 60 / 90 day plan

### First 30 days

- Launch landing page
- Add basic analytics
- Collect family and clinician leads
- Network with East End autism groups and pediatric/community partners
- Interview 15 parents and 10 clinicians
- Confirm legal structure with healthcare counsel

### Days 31–60

- Run a small concierge pilot
- Build secure intake and scheduling workflow
- Create clinician onboarding checklist
- Test AI-assisted intake summaries and caregiver action plan drafts
- Determine private-pay vs insurance path

### Days 61–90

- Convert first paying families if legally ready
- Sign initial provider agreements
- Build repeatable onboarding process
- Track outcomes, satisfaction, and admin time saved
- Prepare investor memo only after there is traction

## Fundraising path

Do not raise big before proving demand.

Step 1: Build MVP and collect proof

- Family leads
- Clinician supply
- Local partnerships
- First revenue or LOIs
- Clear compliance plan

Step 2: Raise a small angel/pre-seed round only if needed

Use of funds:

- Legal/compliance setup
- Clinician onboarding
- HIPAA-safe systems
- Billing/credentialing support
- Local marketing
- Product development

Step 3: Pitch narrative

Harbor Path is building the local-first, clinician-led, AI-assisted autism care access layer for families who need a simpler start into ABA.

## Key risk

The business is promising, but it is healthcare. Do not launch clinical services, collect PHI, or structure provider revenue share without legal and compliance review.
